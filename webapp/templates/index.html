About Brain tumor

A brain tumor is an abnormal growth of cells within the brain or surrounding areas. These cells multiply uncontrollably, forming a mass that can disrupt the delicate functions of the nervous system.

Brain tumors can be benign (non-cancerous) or malignant (cancerous), with varying degrees of growth rate and aggressiveness. Here's a deeper look into brain tumors: Types: There are over 120 different types of brain tumors, classified based on various factors like cell type, growth pattern, and malignancy. Some common types include: Gliomas: The most common type, originating from glial cells that support and nourish neurons. They can range from benign pilocytic astrocytomas to highly aggressive glioblastomas. Meningiomas: Grow from the meninges, membranes surrounding the brain and spinal cord. They're usually benign, slow-growing, and treatable. And other types: Pituitary tumors, Medulloblastomas, Ependymomas.
